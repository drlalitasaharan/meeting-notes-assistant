# Client demo script

## Opening

Today I will show a simple workflow for turning a meeting recording into structured business notes.

The goal is not just transcription. The goal is to make the meeting output useful for follow-up: summary, decisions, next steps, and action items.

## Problem framing

Most teams already have recordings or transcripts, but they still spend time manually converting them into clean notes.

That creates three problems:

1. Important decisions get buried.
2. Action items are easy to miss.
3. Follow-up takes longer than it should.

## Product introduction

Meeting Notes Assistant is designed to take a meeting recording and generate structured notes that are easier to review, share, and act on.

## Live demo flow

First, upload a short meeting recording.

Then the system processes the file and generates notes.

Once processing is complete, open the markdown output and review the main sections.

## What to highlight

1. The output is structured, not just a raw transcript.
2. Decisions are separated from general discussion.
3. Action items are easy to scan.
4. The markdown can be copied into client notes, internal docs, or a project tracker.

## Validation point

The 10-minute demo path was validated separately.

The external-facing markdown passed checks for publishability, including removal of malformed wording and spacing issues.

## Close

The next best step is a controlled pilot with a small number of real meetings.

The pilot should measure:

- Time saved per meeting
- Accuracy of decisions
- Quality of action items
- Whether the output is useful enough to share directly
