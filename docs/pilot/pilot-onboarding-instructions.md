# MeetIQ Pilot Onboarding Instructions

## Purpose

This guide helps early pilot users test MeetIQ with a real meeting recording and provide useful feedback.

MeetIQ turns meeting recordings into structured notes, including:

- Summary
- Key points
- Decisions
- Risks
- Action items
- Next steps

## Recommended First Test

For the first pilot test, use a structured meeting recording that is:

- 5 to 20 minutes long
- Clear audio
- Business or project focused
- Easy to compare against what actually happened in the meeting

Good first examples:

- Client follow-up meeting
- Project planning discussion
- Team sync
- Decision review
- Product or operations discussion

Avoid for the first test:

- Very noisy audio
- Casual conversations with many interruptions
- 60-minute recordings as the first test
- Recordings with highly sensitive information

## How to Use MeetIQ

1. Sign in to MeetIQ.
2. Go to Upload.
3. Upload a meeting recording.
4. Wait for processing to complete.
5. Open the meeting from the Meetings page.
6. Review the generated notes.
7. Copy, edit, download, or share the notes as needed.
8. Send feedback if anything looks incorrect or confusing.

## What to Review

Please check whether MeetIQ correctly captured:

- The main purpose of the meeting
- Important discussion points
- Decisions made
- Risks or blockers
- Action items
- Owners, names, or responsibilities
- Next steps

## What Feedback Is Most Helpful

Helpful feedback includes:

- What worked well
- What was missing
- Any incorrect names, owners, decisions, or action items
- Whether the notes were useful enough to share
- Whether upload or processing was confusing
- Any error messages or stuck processing states

If possible, include:

- Account email
- Meeting title or Meeting ID
- Approximate recording length
- File type
- What you expected
- What actually happened

## Current Pilot Guidance

MeetIQ is currently best suited for short to medium structured meetings.

Recommended pilot recording length:

- Best first test: 5 to 20 minutes
- Good follow-up test: 20 to 30 minutes
- 60-minute meetings: supported on a best-effort basis and should be evaluated further before broader public launch

## Privacy and Sensitive Information

For pilot testing, avoid uploading highly sensitive recordings unless necessary.

Examples to avoid during early pilot:

- Medical information
- Legal matters
- Financial account details
- HR or employee disciplinary discussions
- Confidential client information that should not be used for testing

## Support and Feedback

Use the Support page if:

- Upload fails
- Processing gets stuck
- Notes miss important decisions
- Action items are incorrect or incomplete
- Names, owners, or deadlines are wrong
- Usage limit or pilot access needs review

When reporting an issue, include the meeting title or Meeting ID when possible.
